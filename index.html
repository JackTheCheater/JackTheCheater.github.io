Global Warming

Global warming is the ongoing increase in global average temperature that causes long-term changes to Earth's climate from which it derives the term "Climate Change". This current rise in global average temperature is primarily caused by fossil fuel use, deforestation and some agricultural, industrial and waste management practices that increase the greenhouse effect. What's the greenhouse effect? Here's an animated explanation:

The greenhouse effect is a process that occurs when energy from a planet's host star goes through the planet's atmosphere and heats the planet's surface, but greenhouse gases in the atmosphere prevent most of the heat from returning directly to space, so they make the planet livable with an appropriate temperature for life. The greenhouse gases which play the most significant role are carbon dioxide and methane. Increasing the amount of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere means increasing the greenhouse effect which traps more heat in Earth's lower atmosphere, causing global warming. Ice melting is probably the main indicator of global warming. Here's a video about ice melting in Antarctica:

According to NSF International, Antarctica contains almost 90% of Earth's total ice volume and 70% of its fresh water. The melting of the Arctic and Antarctic ice influences oceanic currents because huge amounts of cold water coming from Antarctica move to hotter oceans slowing down their sea currents and this disrupts the delicate biological balance of the sea ecosystems. The ice melting causes the sea-level rise: if the Arctic should melt completely, the sea level would rise by six meters according to the experts, and a not-negligible amount of land would extinguish. The ice melting leads also to changes in the hydrological cycle, causing frequent periods of droughts.
